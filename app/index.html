The Masked Lords of Waterdeep

Long had the 12 masked lords of Waterdeep ruled from the shadows. A history of benevolence towards the citizens of the city had allowed them to operate without regard for oversight. Their favor spread secretly across the land and much good was attributed to their names, whether or not they actually had a hand in its creation.

The 12 masked lords were akin to their own pantheon of gods. As the people began to believe and trust in them, there seemed no need to continue with the worship of the deities of the land. Temples fell into dissuse and still Waterdeep flourished.

Their prosperity attracted peoples from all over the Sword Coast and beyond. Waterdeep gradually became a melting pot of all kinds of peoples; dragonborn, gnomes, elves, even the occasional tiefling was not uncommon. As more people joined the city the economy stagnated as job creation could not outpace the population growth.

Behind closed doors the 12 masked lords were split on how to best handle the swelling of growth. Their bickering and arguing led to inaction. The people began to grow restless. Native Waterdeepians were going hungry while outsiders took their jobs for less pay. The silence of the masked lords became the rejection of the people by their own gods. Fear crashed into market stalls leaving destruction in the streets.

The Rise of Fing Ch'Nal and the People's Protectorate

Much of what is known of Fing Ch'Nal prior to his rise is contested. Some say he was a powerful mage in the Arcane University of Evocation. Others say he was one of the masked lords and grew sick of their petty squabbles. What is known is that he was influential with the people and he was well funded.

No one in Waterdeep will ever forget when he unmasked the first lord of Waterdeep in the public square. Calling all to look on the face of one of the men they had once revered who had led them to this mess. One by one Fing demystified their lords.

This effort for transparency and call for governmental self direction won much favor with the wizards of Waterdeep. Most of the wizard colleges supported his efforts. The college of illusion was the only major exception, taking a hard stance against these revelations.

Taking advantage of the momentum of the people, Fing setup a political party called the People's Protectorate. His party incited the people's fears and within weeks half of the 12 unmasked lords of Waterdeep had "dissappeared" or been executed in mock public trials. Their sentence "Crimes against the people of Waterdeep", a convenient creation of the People's Protectorate.

Ascension and Exodus

Rallying the masses to his call, "Make Waterdeep Great Again", Fing Ch'Nal asked the people to let him lead them to a glorious new era of peace and prosperity. The people made him their King. The city of Waterdeep was his to rule. The people were excited and calls for action continued.

King Fing Ch'Nal's first royal decree was to call for the registration of all the citizens. At the same time he created jobs through the biggest public works program the city had ever seen, the fortification of Waterdeep. During the three years of the walls construction, the People's Protectorate began to spread lies about plots by the more unusual races to destroy the wall. People began to mistrust neighbors they had known their whole lives, simply because of their race.

On the day of the wall's completion King Fing Ch'Nal ordered all races deemed "not human enough", or NHE to be exiled from the kingdom. A mass exodus began and since that time no one has seen gnomes, dragonborn, tieflings or any other of the NHE races in the Sword Coast. With the decrease in population the prosperity returned and the people slowly forgot their exiled friends. Many justified that it had to be done, "It was us or them".

Paranoia

With no internal enemies present, King Fing Ch'Nal looked outside the walls of Waterdeep for new opponents. Using his Arcane armies, evocation wizards of impressive strength, he quickly swept across all remaining human towns. Insisting that his policies could benefit them as well. If they refused, the devastation left by the Arcane Armies was complete. Baldur's Gate was left a crater of ashe and rubble. Neverwinter was sunk into the Sea of Swords.

The dwarves took note of the utter destruction the King brought upon his own race. As proud as they were they could not subject their people to such obliteration. They agreed to subject their race and cities to the King.

The elves fought many skirmishes and using guerilla warfare tactics were able to avoid complete destruction. They retreated deep into the great woods of the Sword Coast. Upon seeing this the King ordered the High Forest burnt. When the reconnaisance team entered the charred remains of the forest there was sign of the elves, only a scorched forest leading up to the Star Mounts. The King declared that they had been defeated in glorious battle and with this fianl victory extended his rule to include the entire Sword Coast.

Having no enemies left to oppose him, the King in his madness created one from his paranoid mind. His suspicions turned to the organized wizard universities of the Sword Coast. Fearing what they could do if they were to unite and oppose him he ordered the use of all magic illegal. The one exception was wizards with government sanction. Public use of unsanctioned magic in the Sword Coast was punishable by death. The People's Protectorate made sure that the people understood that this was for their safety.

Only three of the wizard universities resisted successfully; the Waxing Moon School of Illusion, the Double Negative School of abjuration, and the Third Eye School of Divination. To this day they operate as an underground network of student and masters of now illegal magical arts.

Legacy

Using his Arcane Archaeologists and Arcane Armies, the King continues to seek all magical power. His goal is to continue to prevent the use of magic by stockpiling it and classifying its secrets. Slowly the world is being drained of magic in the name of peace...

Not all believe in the greatness of the King. There is a small underground movement growing. Most concern themselves with criminal activity and spying. However, in the face of such prosperity there are few that have reason to join this cause. Where will you stand?

Races

Xenophobic nationalism led to a purging of most races not overtly human. This purging was directly requested by the people and facilitated by King Fing Ch'Nal. Playable races still present in Waterdeep include:
    Human Half-elf Halfling Dwarf Changeling

Human

Humans are the most adaptable, flexible, and ambitious people among the common races. They have widely varying tastes, morals, customs, and habits. 90% of humans today live in major cities in the Forgotten Realms; fear of the unknown has curbed their once intrepid curiousity. Having adapted to urban life almost completely most human remember little of their rural roots.

Character building resources for the Human race can be found in the Player's Handbook.

Half-Elf

When the last of the great elven armies were pushed back into the heart of the forests, half-elves were given a choice. Adopt their human parentage wholeheartedly, or be forced out. The ones remaining in the city walls have made their choice. Their subtle elf features are the only reminder of King Fing Ch'Nal's domination of the once mighty elves.

Character building resources for the Half-Elf race can be found in the Player's Handbook.

Halfling

The diminutive halflings survive in a world full of larger creatures by avoiding notice or, barring that, avoiding offense. They appear harmless and so have managed to survive for centuries in the shadow of empires and on the edges of wars and political strife.

Character building resources for the Halfling race can be found in the Player's Handbook.

Dwarf

Boisterous, passionate and economically minded, the dwarves are on of the oldest races on the Sword Coast. After King Fing Ch'Nal negotiated the absorption of all the dwarven cities into the People's Protectorate, the dwarves spread throughout the lands.

Character building resources for the Dwarf race can be found in the Player's Handbook.

Changeling

Changelings can look like anyone at any given time though they do have a true form. Their passive nature has left them with no discernable culture of their own. Their abilities make them able spies and criminals, however they are just as inclined to use their powers for good.

Character building resources for the Changeling race can be found here.

When making your character...

By order of King Fing Ch'Nal: ALL EVOCATION SPELLS HAVE BEEN REMOVED FROM THE SPELL LISTS. Please respect this when selecting spells for your character.

Many new options are available including new classes and new archetypes for your favorite older classes.

You are a part of a criminal underground gang of thieves. Choose your role.

Classes

    Bard Cleric Demolitionist Fighter Monk Ranger Rogue Salve-maker Sorceror Wizard Warlock

Bard

All archetypes from the Player's Handbook are available. In addition a new College of Healing option is also available.

Cleric

Temples and their gods have fallen out of favor in recent times. Some of the old ways are gone and some new have been added. To make a Cleric choose from one of the following domains: Knowledge, Trickery, War, Life (all found in PHB), Martyrdom, Spirit, Undeath

Demolitionist

A master of demolition, he has mastered the art of fighting with explosives. More information found here

Fighter

The Eldritch Knight archetype is no longer available. To replace it the Elite Mercenary archetype has been added.

Monk

The Way of the Four Elements archetype has been removed. Only the Way of the Open Hand and Way of the Shadow are still taught.

Ranger

The Ranger class has been re-tooled. Two new archetypes replace the ones listed in the PHB. They are the Occult Hunter and the Sharpshooter.

Rogue

All Rogue archetypes from the PHB and the Sword Coast Adventurer's guide are available.

Salve-Maker

Mad scientists of potions and poisons. Information found here.

Sorceror

Dragonborn sorcerors were all exiled. The only archetypes remaining are Wild Magic and a new Destined Bloodline.

Warlock

Warlocks are rare in this realm. Otherworldly being have little interest in a world with limited magic. The Fiend pact is no longer available. The Undying pact from the Sword Coast Adventurers Guide is available.

Wizard

Many of the colleges and universities of the wizards were destroyed or scattered to the wind. The college of evocation was coopted into the army. The only Colleges available are Illusion, Divination, and Abjuration.
